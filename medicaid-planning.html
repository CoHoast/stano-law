Understanding Ohio Medicaid

Medicaid is a joint federal and state program that helps cover medical costs, including long-term care, for people with limited income and assets. However, navigating Ohio's Medicaid rules can be complex, and without proper planning, you could lose much of what you've worked a lifetime to build.

At Stano Law Firm, we help families protect their assets while ensuring eligibility for Medicaid benefits. Our experienced team understands the intricacies of Ohio Medicaid law and can develop strategies tailored to your unique situation.

Don't Wait Until It's Too Late

The best time to plan for Medicaid is before you need it. Ohio has a 5-year look-back period for asset transfers, which means planning ahead gives you more options to protect your savings.

Common Questions

Will I lose my home? Not necessarily. Ohio Medicaid rules include protections for your home, especially if your spouse still lives there. We can help you understand your options.

Can I give away my assets? Timing matters. Gifts made within 5 years of applying for Medicaid can result in a penalty period. Proper planning with an experienced attorney is essential.

What about my spouse? Ohio has provisions to protect the "community spouse" from impoverishment. We can help maximize what your spouse keeps.
