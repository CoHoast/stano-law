VA Aid & Attendance

Help veterans and surviving spouses access benefits they've earned to cover long-term care costs.

Benefits You've Earned

The VA Aid & Attendance pension benefit is designed to help wartime veterans and their surviving spouses pay for long-term care—whether at home, in assisted living, or in a nursing home. Unfortunately, many veterans don't know this benefit exists or how to qualify.

At Stano Law Firm, we help veterans and their families navigate the VA benefits process, ensuring they receive the benefits they've earned through their service.

Who Qualifies?

To qualify for VA Aid & Attendance, veterans and surviving spouses must meet certain criteria:

  • Military Service: 90 days of active duty with at least one day during a wartime period
  • Discharge Status: Honorable or general discharge
  • Medical Need: Require assistance with daily activities or are housebound
  • Financial Limits: Meet income and asset thresholds (planning can help)

Wartime Periods

WWII (Dec 7, 1941 – Dec 31, 1946), Korea (June 27, 1950 – Jan 31, 1955), Vietnam (Aug 5, 1964 – May 7, 1975), Gulf War (Aug 2, 1990 – present). You didn't have to serve in combat—just during these periods.

How We Help

  • Eligibility Assessment: Determine if you qualify for VA benefits
  • Asset Planning: Structure assets to meet VA financial requirements
  • Application Assistance: Navigate the complex VA application process
  • Appeals: Help with denied claims or reduced benefits
  • Coordination: Integrate VA benefits with Medicaid planning when appropriate

The Application Process

Applying for VA Aid & Attendance can be complex, with extensive paperwork and strict requirements. Many applications are initially denied due to errors or missing documentation. Working with an experienced attorney helps ensure your application is complete and accurate the first time.

We guide you through every step, from gathering military records to submitting medical evidence and financial documentation.
