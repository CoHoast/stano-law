Navigating Probate in Ohio

When a loved one passes away, their estate often must go through probate—the legal process of validating a will, paying debts, and distributing assets. This process can be confusing and overwhelming, especially while grieving.

At Stano Law Firm, we guide families through every step of probate administration, handling the legal complexities so you can focus on what matters most.

Do You Need Probate?

Not all estates require probate. Assets held in trusts, jointly owned property, and accounts with beneficiary designations often pass outside of probate. We can help you understand what's needed for your situation.

The Probate Process

1

Opening the Estate

File the will with probate court and petition to be appointed as executor or administrator.

2

Inventory Assets

Identify and value all estate assets, from real estate to bank accounts to personal property.

3

Notify Creditors

Publish notice to creditors and handle any legitimate claims against the estate.

4

Pay Debts & Taxes

Settle outstanding debts, file final tax returns, and pay any estate taxes owed.

5

Distribute Assets

Transfer remaining assets to beneficiaries according to the will or Ohio law.
